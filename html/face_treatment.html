Thread Face Lifting

Signs of aging?

One of the most obvious signs of aging is facial drooping. Every day, gravity pulls on the tissues of our faces, and as we age, our skin becomes more floppy. As a result, facial drooping causes an imbalance in our ideal facial proportions by manifesting as hollows, excessive fat, and wrinkles in various places.

Due to extensive research conducted over the years, superior thread materials, and established processes, minimally invasive face lifting procedures like face threading have been progressively gaining wider public approval. Although the process of aging may be unavoidable, there are ways to slow it down. Fortunately, medicine as a science has made enormous strides in discovering strategies to battle the aging process.

Lift your Face and Smile:

Thread Face Lift

For minimally invasive thread lifting, a cannula or needle is used to implant absorbable threads into different parts of the face, chin, or neck. Depending on the type and quantity of threads used, this helps restore our facial features by raising drooping tissues and repositioning facial volumes to achieve a more youthful look.

Threads of various varieties are usually required for various locations. Some threads offer a lift (those with cogs or barbs), others are required to redistribute facial fat, and still others simply stimulate collagen to thicken loose skin (mono threads). The number of threads required will vary depending on the circumstances.

The threads that are implanted work as "reinforcement" for your skin, similar to pillars in a building that provide more volume and structural stability. The material of the threads within the skin also encourages the stimulation of collagen across the area, regenerating it, and initiates the skin's healing reaction.
